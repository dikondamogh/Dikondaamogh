About

Designing with empathy, shipping with rigor.

I'm a product designer who started as a front-end developer — which means I care as much about how a screen feels to build as how it feels to use. Over the last seven years I've partnered with startups and enterprise teams to turn fuzzy problems into interfaces that are clear, accessible, and genuinely enjoyable.

My process blends structured research with fast, scrappy prototyping. I'm happiest in the messy middle — reframing a brief, testing an idea with real users, then refining it until it disappears into the experience. Outside of client work, I mentor early-career designers and write about design systems.

Case studies

A closer look at the process

Expand any project below to see the problem, the process, and the measurable result.

Problem

Customers found the analytics dashboard powerful but overwhelming — key metrics were buried three clicks deep, and support tickets about "finding the right report" were rising.

Process

I ran 12 user interviews, mapped the top five reporting journeys, and tested three low-fidelity layouts before landing on a card-based dashboard with saved views.

Solution

A configurable dashboard with role-based defaults, inline chart annotations, and a search-first navigation model that replaced a five-level menu tree.

-40%Time to insight
-58%Support tickets
+22ptNPS

Problem

Early testers described the app's streak-based system as "guilt-inducing," causing people to abandon the app after missing a single day.

Process

Diary studies over two weeks revealed that flexible, compassionate framing mattered more than gamified pressure. I prototyped three alternative feedback models.

Solution

Replaced hard streak counters with a gentle "momentum" visualization that forgives missed days, paired with softer copy and color throughout.

+31%30-day retention
-45%Post-miss dropout
4.8App Store rating

Problem

Six product teams were each maintaining their own button, input, and modal components — resulting in inconsistent UI and slow engineering handoff.

Process

I audited 340 existing components, ran alignment workshops with each team's lead designer, and built a token architecture that could flex across brands.

Solution

A documented, Storybook-linked system of 128 components and design tokens, with a contribution model that let teams propose new patterns.

-50%Handoff time
6Teams adopted
128Shared components
